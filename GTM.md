# GTM.md — Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or CTO at a 5–30 person seed/Series A startup. They approve or directly pay SaaS bills. They gave every engineer Cursor Pro when it launched, added Claude Team for the writing folks, and now they're looking at a $3k+/month AI bill wondering if it's worth it. They're not cheap — they're budget-conscious in the way that anyone with a burn rate is budget-conscious.

**Secondary:** Founder-engineers at solo/micro startups who are paying for 2–3 AI tools personally and suspect they're overlapping.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1: Seed with authentic posts**
- Post the tool on Hacker News as "Show HN: Free tool to audit your AI tool spend." Time it for 9am ET Tuesday. Don't oversell. Let the audit results speak.
- Cross-post to r/ExperiencedDevs and r/SoftwareEngineering with a framing like "I kept seeing $2k+/month AI bills at companies with 5 devs — built a free auditor."
- Post the most surprising real audit result (anonymized) as a tweet thread. "I audited a 6-person team's AI spend. They were paying $1,800/month. Here's what I found." Hook with the number, deliver the breakdown.

**Week 2: Community seeding**
- Find every thread in the last 3 months where someone asked about Cursor vs Copilot pricing. Reply with a link + one-line result. Don't spam — add value first, link second.
- DM 20 indie hackers on X who post about their expenses/tools. "I built something that might be useful — here's what it found for a 3-person team similar to yours." Personalize with a real audit.

**Week 3: Product Hunt + newsletter pitches**
- Submit to Product Hunt. The shareable result URL with OG preview is designed for this — the screenshot of "$847/month in savings" is the thumbnail.
- Pitch 3 developer-focused newsletters: TLDR Dev, Bytes.dev, The Pragmatic Engineer. Cold email with the HN post as social proof. One link, one sentence, no deck.

**Week 4: Retention and referral loop**
- Email everyone who captured their audit. "We updated pricing data for ChatGPT Team — your audit changed. Here's what's new." Brings them back.
